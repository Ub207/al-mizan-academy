Q: What is Tajweed?
A: Tajweed literally means "beautification" or "improvement." In Quran recitation, Tajweed refers to the set of rules governing how each letter should be pronounced, including its articulation point (Makhraj), characteristics (Sifaat), and how letters interact with each other. Proper Tajweed ensures you recite the Quran exactly as it was revealed to Prophet Muhammad (PBUH). It is considered Fard (obligatory) for every Muslim to recite with basic Tajweed.

Q: What is Saba Qiraat?
A: Saba Qiraat (also called the Seven Qira'at) are the seven authentic styles of Quran recitation transmitted through unbroken chains from the Prophet Muhammad (PBUH). Each Qira'ah has slight variations in pronunciation, vowelization, and recitation style, but all convey the same divine message. The seven Qurra (reciters) are: Nafi', Ibn Kathir, Abu Amr, Ibn Amir, Asim, Hamzah, and Al-Kisa'i. Very few scholars worldwide are qualified to teach all seven, making this a rare and prestigious specialization.

Q: What is Ijazah?
A: Ijazah is a certificate of authority and permission granted by a qualified scholar to a student, confirming that the student has mastered Quran recitation and can now teach and transmit the Quran to others. It includes a complete Sanad (chain of narration) going back to the Prophet Muhammad (PBUH). Our Ijazah program is for advanced students who have completed Hifz and Tajweed.
